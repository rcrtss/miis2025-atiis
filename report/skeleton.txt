Methods

  M1 — Context & scope (thin — motivation only)
  - Only in committee example. 
  - Focus on p0 sweep for main analysis, but consider other sweeps for future work or in an appendix.
  - Focus on 3 different LLMs for main analysis, but consider more for future work or in an appendix.
  - Expected to diverge from original paper's behavior, and consider surfice actions and parameters as secondary validation axes for understanding LLMs' blame judgments.

  M2 — Reimplementation
  - Re-implementation of original paper's method on the committee example to replicate results and do ablation studies for other combinations of values of the parameters.
  
  M3 — LLM evaluation
  - A research harness probing whether LLMs judge blame by the counterfactual structure of a collective decision (who was pivotal, at what cost) or just by surface action (voted no = blame). The formal ground truth comes from a Shapley-blame framework (framework/committee.py, Friedenberg–Halpern 2019).
  - Skinning method: use 3 different scenarios that are isomorphic to the committee example, but with different surface actions (e.g., voting yes/no, donating/not donating, etc.) to see if LLMs judge blame based on the counterfactual structure or just surface actions. For each scenario, 3 phrasings for parameters (e.g., yes/no, alpha value, cost) to see if LLMs are sensitive to the parameters of the problem or just surface actions (currently indexed to 0, not random).
  - Use 3 different LLMs to see if there are differences in how they judge blame.
  - Single sweep considered for focus in the analysis: p0. why? because it is the most intuitive and has the most clear-cut counterfactual structure (pivotal agent is the one who votes no, and the cost of action is 1). Other sweeps can be considered for future work or in an appendix.
  - Ablation studies: vary the parameters of the committee example (e.g., yes/no, alpha value, cost) to see how it affects LLMs' blame judgments. (using alpha, c_sw×N, and vote as secondary validation axes on the p0 sweep)
